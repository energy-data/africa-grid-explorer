About the Africa Electricity Grids Explorer

The World Bank has created the Africa Electricity Grids Explorer as a way to navigate the most up to date collection of open data on grid networks in Sub-Saharan Africa. This intends to support initiatives in grid and off-grid electricity access, grid infrastructure upgrading, renewable energy and sector planning. It is also part of a push to increase the openness and accessibility of energy data for Sustainable Development Goals, building on the ENERGYDATA.INFO platform, along with platforms like World Bank Open Data.

This dataset is based on a wide variety of sources, some already open, some open but hard to find, and many that have not been publicly available before. Apart from highlighting the important progress that has been made in opening grid energy-related data globally, this tool also serves to identify gaps in data availability and helps inform further efforts in the space.

Note on limitations: This data is incomplete and the sources vary by country and region. It should not be used to make comparisons about the extent of grid coverage between countries or areas. For more information and copyright information, please visit the full source dataset on ENERGYDATA.INFO. Please also explore the many other datasets and apps that have been made available on this platform.

Please contact our team at energydata@worldbankgroup.org if you are interested in collaborating further.
